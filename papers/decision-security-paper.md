**Author:** Tharcilia Rollemberg

# Abstract

The emergence of autonomous AI systems is transforming organizational decision-making processes.

Traditional cybersecurity frameworks focus on protecting infrastructure, applications, and information assets. However, autonomous systems increasingly influence recommendations, prioritization, workflow execution, and strategic outcomes.

This shift introduces a new challenge: protecting decisions themselves.

This paper proposes the concept of **Decision Security**, a discipline focused on preserving the integrity, reliability, accountability, and trustworthiness of decisions produced or influenced by autonomous AI systems.

The paper argues that decisions are becoming critical organizational assets and may emerge as a primary target for future cyber attacks.

Keywords: Autonomous AI, AI Security, Decision Integrity, Decision Trust, Agentic AI, Digital Trust.

---

# 1. Introduction

Artificial Intelligence is transitioning from a tool that assists users to a capability that increasingly participates in decision-making.

Modern organizations are deploying AI systems to:

* Recommend actions
* Prioritize resources
* Detect threats
* Support financial decisions
* Assist executives
* Automate operational workflows

As organizations delegate authority to autonomous systems, decisions become increasingly dependent on machine reasoning.

This transformation raises a fundamental question:

> How can organizations protect decisions generated by autonomous systems?

Traditional cybersecurity frameworks provide extensive mechanisms for protecting systems and data, but they provide limited guidance for protecting the integrity of autonomous decisions.

This paper proposes Decision Security as a new cybersecurity discipline designed to address this challenge.

# 3. The Rise of Decision-Centric Risk

Historically, attackers targeted:

* Infrastructure
* Applications
* Data

Autonomous AI systems introduce a new target:

```text
Data
 ↓
Context
 ↓
Reasoning
 ↓
Decision
 ↓
Action
```

Manipulating any stage of this process may influence the final decision.

In many cases, the decision itself becomes more valuable than the system generating it.

---

# 4. Defining Decision Security

## Proposed Definition

Decision Security is the discipline responsible for protecting the integrity, reliability, accountability, and trustworthiness of decisions produced or influenced by autonomous systems.

The objective is to ensure that decisions remain:

* Accurate
* Aligned
* Explainable
* Governed
* Trustworthy

---

## Core Principles

### Decision Integrity

Decisions should remain accurate and resistant to manipulation.

### Decision Alignment

Decisions should support organizational objectives.

### Decision Transparency

Reasoning should be understandable.

### Decision Accountability

Ownership and responsibility should be identifiable.

### Decision Trustworthiness

Stakeholders should maintain justified confidence.

---

# 5. Decision-Centric Threat Model

This paper proposes five categories of decision threats.

| Category               | Description                                     |
| ---------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- |
| Decision Manipulation  | Deliberate influence of outcomes                |
| Decision Drift         | Progressive deviation from objectives           |
| Hallucinated Decisions | Decisions based on fabricated information       |
| Confidence Inflation   | Overstated confidence in outputs                |
| Decision Misalignment  | Decisions conflicting with goals or regulations |

---

## Example 1: Prompt Injection

An attacker manipulates context to influence recommendations.

Result:

The infrastructure remains secure, but the decision becomes compromised.

---

## Example 2: Context Poisoning

Compromised information sources influence reasoning.

Result:

Incorrect decisions despite technically functioning systems.

---

# 6. Decision Integrity Model

Decision Integrity becomes the primary security objective.

The proposed model evaluates:

| Dimension      | Objective                   |
| -------------- | --------------------------- |
| Accuracy       | Correctness of decisions    |
| Consistency    | Stability over time         |
| Explainability | Transparency of reasoning   |
| Accountability | Ownership of outcomes       |
| Alignment      | Consistency with objectives |

Together, these dimensions form the Decision Integrity Index.

# 9. Discussion

Autonomous AI systems are changing the role of cybersecurity.

Traditional security focused on protecting infrastructure.

Future security programs may increasingly focus on protecting:

* Decisions
* Reasoning
* Confidence
* Trust

This evolution does not replace existing security disciplines.

Instead, it extends them.

Decision Security complements AI Security by addressing risks associated with decision-making processes rather than technical assets alone.

# 11. Conclusion

Autonomous AI systems introduce a new category of organizational risk.

As intelligent systems become increasingly involved in business-critical processes, protecting infrastructure alone will no longer be sufficient.

Organizations must also protect the integrity of decisions.

Decision Security proposes a new perspective for cybersecurity—one that recognizes decisions as critical assets requiring governance, monitoring, assurance, and protection.

In autonomous environments, the most valuable target may no longer be the system.

It may be the decision itself.

---

# Research Proposition

> The next generation of cyber attacks will increasingly target decisions rather than systems. Organizations that learn to protect decision-making processes will be better positioned to operate trustworthy autonomous AI systems.
