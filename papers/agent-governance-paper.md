# Agent Governance: Governance Models for Autonomous AI Agents

**Author:** Tharcilia Rollemberg

---

# Abstract

The rise of autonomous AI agents is transforming how organizations make decisions, execute workflows, and manage business operations.

Unlike traditional software systems, autonomous agents possess varying levels of decision-making authority, contextual reasoning capabilities, and operational autonomy.

As organizations increasingly delegate responsibilities to intelligent agents, governance becomes a critical challenge.

This paper proposes Agent Governance as a discipline focused on establishing accountability, oversight, authorization, and trust mechanisms for autonomous AI systems.

The paper introduces governance principles, authority models, governance architectures, and accountability structures designed to support trustworthy autonomy in enterprise environments.

Keywords: AI Governance, Autonomous Agents, Agentic AI, Digital Trust, Autonomous Systems, Governance, Risk and Compliance (GRC).

---

# 1. Introduction

Artificial Intelligence is evolving beyond traditional software paradigms.

Modern AI agents can:

* Analyze information
* Generate recommendations
* Execute workflows
* Interact with enterprise systems
* Collaborate with other agents
* Influence business outcomes

These capabilities create a new category of organizational actor.

Historically, governance frameworks were designed for:

* Humans
* Processes
* Systems
* Data

They were not designed for autonomous entities capable of decision-making and action execution.

This paper addresses the emerging challenge of governing autonomous AI agents.

# 3. The Agent Governance Problem

Organizations increasingly deploy agents capable of:

* Executing tasks
* Making recommendations
* Taking actions
* Interacting with external systems

This introduces governance questions such as:

### Who owns the agent?

### Who is accountable for its decisions?

### What authority should the agent possess?

### How should autonomous actions be audited?

### What level of oversight is required?

These questions define the Agent Governance Problem.

---

# 4. Defining Agent Governance

## Proposed Definition

Agent Governance is the discipline responsible for establishing policies, controls, accountability structures, and oversight mechanisms for autonomous AI agents.

Its objective is to ensure that agents operate in alignment with organizational goals, regulatory requirements, risk tolerances, and ethical principles.

---

## Core Question

> How can organizations safely delegate authority to autonomous AI agents?

# 5. Principles of Agent Governance

The paper proposes six foundational principles.

---

## Accountability

Every agent must have identifiable ownership.

---

## Transparency

Agent behavior should be observable and explainable.

---

## Authorization

Agent authority must be explicitly defined.

---

## Oversight

Human supervision should be proportional to risk.

---

## Compliance

Agents should operate within legal and organizational requirements.

---

## Trustworthiness

Agents should preserve stakeholder confidence.

# 6. Agent Authority Model

Agent authority should be formally defined.

The paper proposes a five-level authority model.

| Level   | Description              |
| ------- | ------------------------ |
| Level 0 | Advisory Only            |
| Level 1 | Recommendation Authority |
| Level 2 | Assisted Execution       |
| Level 3 | Conditional Autonomy     |
| Level 4 | Autonomous Execution     |
| Level 5 | Strategic Autonomy       |

Higher levels require stronger governance controls.

# 17. Conclusion

The increasing autonomy of AI systems creates one of the most significant governance challenges of the digital era.

Organizations can no longer rely solely on governance models designed for people, processes, and traditional software systems.

Autonomous agents require dedicated governance structures capable of ensuring accountability, oversight, authorization, and trust.

Agent Governance provides a foundation for addressing this challenge and enabling trustworthy autonomy within modern enterprises.
