# References

## Overview

The Trust-Centric Threat Modeling Framework (TCTM) builds upon established research and industry standards spanning cybersecurity, artificial intelligence, governance, risk management, trustworthy AI, and autonomous systems.

The following references provide the theoretical and practical foundations that informed the development of the framework.

# Research Gap

The existing body of research provides substantial coverage of:

* Cybersecurity
* AI Governance
* AI Safety
* Adversarial Machine Learning
* Responsible AI

However, relatively little work has focused on:

* Trust as a measurable security objective
* Decision Integrity as a cybersecurity concern
* Trust-aware threat modeling
* Trust observability
* Trust maturity assessment for autonomous systems

The Trust-Centric Threat Modeling Framework (TCTM) seeks to address this gap by integrating trust, governance, decision integrity, and cybersecurity into a unified framework for autonomous AI systems.
