# Abstract

The rapid adoption of autonomous AI agents is transforming how organizations operate, make decisions, and execute business processes.

Unlike traditional software systems, autonomous agents possess varying degrees of decision-making authority, contextual reasoning, and operational autonomy.

As organizations increasingly delegate responsibilities to intelligent agents, a critical question emerges:

> Who governs autonomous AI agents?

This research note proposes Agent Governance as an emerging discipline focused on establishing accountability, oversight, authorization, and control mechanisms for autonomous AI systems.

The central hypothesis is that organizations will eventually require dedicated governance structures for managing autonomous agents, similar to existing governance models for cybersecurity, risk management, and corporate compliance.

---

# Introduction

Organizations have historically developed governance mechanisms for every critical business capability.

Examples include:

* Corporate Governance
* IT Governance
* Data Governance
* Cybersecurity Governance
* Risk Governance

These governance structures exist because organizational assets require oversight.

Autonomous AI introduces a new category of organizational actor.

Unlike traditional software, autonomous agents can:

* Make recommendations
* Prioritize actions
* Execute workflows
* Interact with systems
* Collaborate with other agents
* Influence business outcomes

This shift creates a new governance challenge.

---

# The Governance Gap

Most organizations currently govern:

| Asset                | Governance Model           |
| -------------------- | -------------------------- |
| Employees            | Human Resources Governance |
| Data                 | Data Governance            |
| Applications         | IT Governance              |
| Cybersecurity        | Security Governance        |
| Financial Activities | Corporate Governance       |

However, autonomous agents often operate between these domains.

As a result, governance responsibilities become unclear.

This creates what may be described as the **Agent Governance Gap**.

---

# Defining Agent Governance

## Proposed Definition

Agent Governance is the discipline responsible for establishing policies, controls, accountability mechanisms, and oversight structures for autonomous AI agents.

Its objective is to ensure that autonomous systems operate in alignment with organizational goals, regulatory requirements, ethical principles, and acceptable risk thresholds.

---

## Core Question

> How can organizations safely delegate authority to autonomous agents?

---

# Why Agent Governance Matters

The level of autonomy granted to AI systems continues to increase.

Organizations are deploying agents capable of:

* Executing transactions
* Managing workflows
* Conducting investigations
* Producing recommendations
* Supporting strategic decisions

As autonomy increases, so does organizational dependence.

Without governance, organizations may lose visibility and control over critical decisions.

# Governance Dimensions

## Strategic Governance

Focus:

Alignment with business objectives.

Questions:

* Why does the agent exist?
* What business outcomes should it support?

---

## Operational Governance

Focus:

Day-to-day operation.

Questions:

* How does the agent behave?
* Is performance acceptable?

---

## Security Governance

Focus:

Protection against misuse and compromise.

Questions:

* Can the agent be manipulated?
* Are controls effective?

---

## Decision Governance

Focus:

Quality and integrity of decisions.

Questions:

* Can decisions be trusted?
* Are recommendations aligned?

---

## Compliance Governance

Focus:

Regulatory and policy adherence.

Questions:

* Does the agent comply with legal requirements?
* Are controls auditable?

Organizations are rapidly moving toward Agentic AI architectures.

---

# Conclusion

The rise of autonomous AI agents introduces a new governance challenge for modern organizations.

Traditional governance models were designed for people, processes, and systems.

They were not designed for intelligent entities capable of making decisions and executing actions.

Agent Governance proposes a framework for addressing this challenge by establishing accountability, oversight, authorization, and trust mechanisms for autonomous systems.

As organizations increasingly delegate authority to AI agents, governance may become the most important control mechanism for trustworthy autonomy.

---

# Research Proposition

> Organizations will eventually govern autonomous AI agents with the same rigor applied to financial systems, cybersecurity programs, and corporate risk management. Agent Governance will emerge as a foundational capability for operating trustworthy autonomous enterprises.
