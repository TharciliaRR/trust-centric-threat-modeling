# Research Contributions

## Trust-Centric Threat Modeling Framework (TCTM)

### Version 1.0

---

# Purpose

This document summarizes the primary research contributions introduced by the Trust-Centric Threat Modeling Framework (TCTM).

The objective is to clearly identify the conceptual, methodological, and practical innovations proposed by the framework.

---

# Overview

Traditional threat modeling frameworks focus on protecting systems, applications, infrastructure, and data.

TCTM introduces a trust-oriented perspective designed for autonomous AI systems, where decisions, governance, accountability, and confidence become critical organizational assets.

The framework proposes several novel concepts intended to support the evaluation and governance of trustworthy autonomy.

---

# Contribution 1 — Trust Domains Model

## Description

TCTM introduces a structured trust evaluation model composed of five trust domains.

### Trust Domains

| Domain            | Objective                                              |
| ----------------- | ------------------------------------------------------ |
| Identity Trust    | Trust in identities, authentication, and authorization |
| Context Trust     | Trust in information and knowledge sources             |
| Decision Trust    | Trust in recommendations and autonomous decisions      |
| Governance Trust  | Trust in accountability and oversight                  |
| Operational Trust | Trust in runtime behavior and resilience               |

---

## Contribution

Provides a multidimensional framework for evaluating trustworthiness in autonomous AI systems.

# Contribution 3 — Trust Impact Dimension

## Description

Traditional risk models typically evaluate:

```text
Risk = Impact × Likelihood
```

TCTM introduces an additional factor:

```text
Risk = Impact × Likelihood × Trust Impact
```

---

## Contribution

Establishes trust degradation as an explicit risk variable.

This enables organizations to evaluate risks not only by operational damage but also by their effect on confidence and trustworthiness.

---

# Contribution 4 — Decision Security

## Description

TCTM introduces the concept of Decision Security.

### Definition

Decision Security is the discipline of protecting the integrity, reliability, accountability, and trustworthiness of autonomous decisions.

---

## Contribution

Expands cybersecurity beyond system protection and proposes decision protection as a new security objective for autonomous AI systems.

# Strategic Contribution

The central contribution of TCTM is the proposition that:

> Trust should be treated as a measurable, governable, and protectable organizational capability.

Rather than viewing trust as a byproduct of secure systems, TCTM positions trust as a primary objective of autonomous AI governance and cybersecurity.

---

# Research Proposition

> The next evolution of cybersecurity will not be defined solely by protecting systems, but by protecting trust in autonomous decision-making systems.

TCTM provides a foundational framework for understanding, measuring, governing, and preserving that trust.
